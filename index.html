Technical skills

As most modern day developers, I started my introduction to Software by studying the foundations of HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. With the help of online resources, I was able to grasp an idea of the functionality of Software Development.


I embarked on my bootcamp Master Code course with Code Nation, September '20 where I was taught in depth the power of JavaScript, a wider range of elements I could use in HTML and even more creative aesthetics with CSS. As the weeks went by, we were introduced to a new framework - React. From then on I have always had a keen insight into the functionality and fluidity of how React works and to this moment, I still favour React as my preferred framework.


Looking at the MERN (Mongo, Express, React, Node) stack, it was a little overwhelming at first, but after further study and work using Mongo and Express, our team were able to build a fully functioning fullstack app. I enjoyed working on the backend with Mongo, and linking the front and backend together using Express.



Around week 9 of the Bootcamp, we looked at Python and Django. A brand new language for me where I grasped the 'easy to write + read' syntax and powerful practicality. Using Python and Django, our teams were able to build fullstack apps. I thoroughly enjoyed working with Django and how productive the built in admin resource was.

During the entirety of the course, we learnt and used helpful tools to assist us with our projects. With the introduction to Docker, I was able to understand the use of containers and images and the functionality of connecting them to a Database. Using an application called Insomnia, I was able to test backend code quickly and efficiently. In order to meet industry expectations, we used GitKraken to comply with our Git repositories. This was an essential skill, as it was necessary when working with teams, in order to know who was working on what and to review then approve pull requests when a file was completed before merging.

I graduated from Code Nation in December '20 having completed 12 weeks of intense learning. I am grateful for the curriculum taught but also for the other skills I developed along the way. Through apps such as Slack, communication and team work were vital in order to meet deadlines and to successfully achieve the criteria. From lightning talks and retro presentations, these are skills that have also benefitted me.
I now aim to better myself further through more self study to push my knowledge and capabilites to a higher level. Please feel free to browse through the Projects listed on the Projects page.
